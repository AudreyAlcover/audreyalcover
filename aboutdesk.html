I started by studying plastic art at the university then I turned to management in order to acquire commercial skills.

During my BTEC in Management in work-study, I worked for two years at the Musée Grévin in Paris as a shop assistant.

After opting for my diploma I decided to follow the course of a bachelor in luxury marketing, where I did an internship at Prada for the shoe brand Church's. It is following the option of my bachelor that I went on an adventure in Australia for two years.

During my trip to Australia, I encountered many existential questions but also the vital interest of communication. I have always been passionate about design but I did not yet know which path to study to make it my future job.
After going around Australia in a van, having worked in different cities, I used many web platforms and applications to simplify my daily research (find a job, find accommodation, find where to go out but also places to visit). On returning to France I was invited to pass a competition in the IronHack school.I passed this competition successfully and therefore I studied Design UX / UI.

This job is particularly fascinating to me because it continues to evolve,it is by doing reches that we discover new needs and therefore new design.
